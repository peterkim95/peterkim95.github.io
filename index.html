A Timeline About Myself

I guarantee that you've never seen a timeline as interesting as mine.

Started Breathing

I was born in Asan Hospital in Gangnam, Seoul, South Korea. My official Korean name is Taek-Sang Kim (김택상). Peter is fine, too.

December 1st, 1995

Moved to Paris, France

After 9 months I was born, I took my first airplane ride and moved to Paris, France. My first memory happens as I sip milk from my favorite mug, looking at the view of the Eiffel Tower at night, from the veranda of our apartment. Not a bad first memory.

September 1996 - August 1998

Moved to Tehran, Iran

People are shocked when they hear this and they always ask "How bad was it?". Then I remind them that it was before 5 months before 9/11. It's interesting because I remember it as a very peaceful place filled with kind people. Sure my mom had to wear a Hijab when she was outside, but other than that, my family was undisturbed by the community. One of my fondest memories was going to the local bootleg VHS store, and renting out Tarzan or Mulan with the option of English or Farsi dubbed (I chose English). Another really weird fact: I went to an Italian kindergarten and learnt how to count 1 to 10 in Italian really well. Sadly, I don't remember it anymore.

August 1998 - July 2001

Moved back to Seoul, Korea

I realized that my first memory of Korea starts here, actually. That's how young I was when I first moved to Paris. I was back at an awesome time because there was so much excitement and energy everywhere because of the 2002 World Cup, at which Korea made it to the semi-finals for the first time in history.

August 2001 - July 2003

Moved to Brussels, Belgium

Started off a little rocky because of the huge culture change and language problems. But eventually found my groove as I could finally communicate with my teachers in fluent English, and most importantly, make friends. It's always easier to fit in when you're younger.

August 2003 - February 2005

Moved to Geneva, Switzerland

Literally lived 5 mins away from the United Nations HQ. It was tough leaving this place because I genuinely made great friends. (Luckily, I have them on Facebook now, and we stay in touch)

March 2005 - February 2007

Moved to Gangnam, Seoul, Korea

Started off rocky again because of the culture readjustments. Again, found my groove eventually because I am pretty good at this adapting to new social situations quite literally every 2 or three years. When I was here, I had my first dog; his name was Tino. Here's me and him napping together.
